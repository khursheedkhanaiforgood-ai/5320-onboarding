Lesson from Mar 27: After setting static routes on Quantum Fiber, internet still failed because ipforwarding was OFF on the Default VLAN — return traffic arrived at 192.168.0.x but could not be routed to 10.30.x.x. Must run enable ipforwarding vlan <name> on every routed VLAN interface, not just the data VLANs. This will be a checklist item in Module C.

Open Questions — Answer These Before Module B

Question 1 — Physical Topology
How are Switch_1 and Switch_2 physically connected to each other and to the Quantum Fiber modem? Draw it out: does each switch have its own cable to the modem, or does Switch_2 daisy-chain through Switch_1?
Hint: your answer changes how many uplink ports you need, how many static routes the modem needs, and whether VLAN 10 needs a routed trunk or just a tagged port.
Question 2 — Management VLAN 10
You said VLAN 10 should "somehow connect both switches." What do you want to be able to do from a management workstation — reach both switches on a single subnet, or just ensure the switches can talk to each other?
Hint: if you want one SSH session to reach both 10.10.0.1 and 10.10.0.2, the trunk link between the switches must carry VLAN 10 tagged.
Question 3 — Static Routes
How many static routes will the Quantum Fiber modem need, and what is the next-hop IP for each? Think about which device is the gateway for each subnet and whether the modem can reach it directly.
Hint: you have four data subnets across two switches. The modem only knows 192.168.0.0/24 directly.
Question 4 — Port Assignment
Which port on each switch is the uplink to the home router? The 5320-16P-2MXT-2X has ports 1–16 plus multi-XG uplinks. Port 1 was used last time — is that your plan again for both switches?
Hint: the uplink port carries untagged Default VLAN traffic for ZTP+ and XIQ. It should be a different port from your AP ports (3, 5, 13, 15).
Question 5 — AP Reset
How are you planning to factory reset the AP3000s? XIQ has a reset option, the AP has a physical reset button, and EXOS has CLI commands to decommission. Which method will you use?
Hint: if XIQ still shows the APs as adopted, a physical button reset alone may not remove them from the XIQ inventory. Check the XIQ decommission flow first.
